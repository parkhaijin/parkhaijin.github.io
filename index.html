About

I am a Professor of Law at Hanyang University School of Law, where I also serve as Director of the AI and Law Research Center. My research lies at the intersection of law and economics, computational law, and empirical legal studies. I apply quantitative and computational methods—including natural language processing, machine learning, and experimental design—to problems in civil liability, AI governance, and legal decision-making.

I received my J.S.D. and J.S.M. from Stanford Law School (Ph.D. minor in Computer Science) and a B.A. in Law from Seoul National University. Before joining academia, I practiced as an attorney at Kim & Chang and served as a judge in the Korean judiciary.
